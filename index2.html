Ocean Poem

the new collection by

George Murray

Murray's compositions are often delicate and evocative vignettes. But then, there can be percussive outbursts of passion. They breathe harsh winter, cold Maine beaches, the gentle tranquility of quiet friendship. They echo with remorse, with patience, with awe for an endless, living sea. They speak of autumn reverie in an abandoned church, of thinking silently about one you really loved, years since you've spoken to them.
as featured on NPR - Echoes

George is a mysterious and migrant spirit. For half the year, he works as an interior painter in Massachusetts, doing odd jobs here and there. For the summer and autumn months, George plays caretaker to a secluded estate nested in the blueberry barrens of northern Maine.
